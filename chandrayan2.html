Chandrayaan-2

Chandrayaan-2 is India's second lunar exploration mission, launched by ISRO in July 2019. Its goal was to deliver an orbiter, a lander (Vikram), and a rover (Pragyan) to the Moon, with a particular focus on the Moon's southern hemisphere. Although the lander failed to achieve a soft landing, the orbiter remains operational and continues to study the Moon from orbit.

Key Components

  • Orbiter: Equipped with advanced instruments, it is expected to operate for seven years, collecting data on the Moon's surface, mineral composition, and searching for signs of water.
  • Vikram Lander: Named after Vikram Sarabhai, the founder of India's space program, it was designed to touch down near the Moon's south pole, but the landing attempt failed.
  • Pragyan Rover: Carried by Vikram, it was supposed to explore the surface for one lunar day (14 Earth days), studying the Moon's composition and potential water ice.

Orbiter Instruments

  • Terrain Mapping Camera 2 (TMC 2): Creates high-resolution 3D maps of the lunar surface.
  • Chandrayaan-2 Large Area Soft X-ray Spectrometer (CLASS): Analyzes X-ray fluorescence to detect elements on the Moon's surface.
  • Solar X-ray Monitor (XSM): Measures solar X-rays, aiding the interpretation of XRF data.
  • Orbiter High Resolution Camera (OHRC): Captures highly detailed images of the lunar surface, especially around potential landing sites.
  • Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR): Detects water ice in permanently shadowed craters and maps the lunar regolith.
  • Imaging Infrared Spectrometer (IIRS): Maps and characterizes hydroxyl and water molecules in the Moon's polar regions.
  • Chandra Atmospheric Composition Explorer 2 (ChACE-2): Samples the Moon's tenuous atmosphere to study its composition.
  • Dual Frequency Radio Science (DFRS) Experiment: Investigates the electron density in the lunar ionosphere.

Mission Objectives

Chandrayaan-2 aims to build upon the success of Chandrayaan-1, using improved technology to map the Moon's topography, study surface minerals and elements, explore the lunar exosphere, and search for water ice. Despite the lander not successfully reaching the surface, the mission's orbiter continues to make valuable contributions to lunar science.

Mission Cost

The mission cost approximately ₹603 crore ($87 million).

Chandrayaan-2 Mission Details

Mission Lunar Exploration, Planetary Science
Weight 3850 kg (Mass at lift-off)
Onboard Power 750 Watts
Stabilization 3-axis stabilized using reaction wheels and attitude control thrusters, sun sensors, and star sensors for attitude determination.
Payloads Scientific Payloads from India:
  • a) Terrain Mapping Camera 2 (TMC 2)
  • b) Chandrayaan-2 Large Area Soft X-ray Spectrometer (CLASS)
  • c) Solar X-ray Monitor (XSM)
  • d) Imaging Infrared Spectrometer (IIRS)
  • e) Dual Frequency Radio Science (DFRS) Experiment
Scientific Payloads from Abroad:
  • f) None (all payloads were developed in India)
Launch Date 22 July 2019
Launch Site SDSC, SHAR, Sriharikota
Launch Vehicle GSLV Mk III-M1
Orbit 100 km x 100 km : Lunar Orbit
Mission Life 7 years (Orbiter)
