Chandrayaan-1

Chandrayaan-1, India's first mission to the Moon, was successfully launched on October 22, 2008, from SDSC SHAR, Sriharikota. This landmark mission aimed to study the Moon's surface with a focus on chemical, mineralogical, and topographical analysis. It carried instruments developed by India and international partners (USA, UK, Germany, Sweden, and Bulgaria).

Key Milestones

  • **Launch and Orbital Maneuvers:** Successfully raised its orbit through a series of burns, entering lunar orbit.
  • **Moon Impact Probe (MIP):** Released a probe that crashed into the Moon near the Shackleton Crater, providing early evidence of water in the lunar atmosphere.
  • **Scientific Contributions:** Detected hydroxyl and water-bearing molecules, challenging previous beliefs and opening new avenues for lunar research.
  • **Challenges:** Faced overheating and sensor failures, concluding the mission in August 2009 after completing 95% of its objectives.

Chandrayaan-1 Mission Details

Mission Remote Sensing, Planetary Science
Weight 1380 kg (Mass at lift-off)
Onboard Power 700 Watts
Stabilization 3-axis stabilized using reaction wheel and attitude control thrusters, sun sensors, star sensors, fibre optic gyros, and accelerometers for attitude determination.
Payloads Scientific Payloads from India:
  • a) Terrain Mapping Camera (TMC)
  • b) Hyper Spectral Imager (HySI)
  • c) Lunar Laser Ranging Instrument (LLRI)
  • d) High Energy X-ray Spectrometer (HEX)
  • e) Moon Impact Probe (MIP)
Scientific Payloads from Abroad:
  • f) Chandrayaan-I X-ray Spectrometer (CIXS)
  • g) Near Infrared Spectrometer (SIR - 2)
  • h) Sub keV Atom Reflecting Analyzer (SARA)
  • i) Miniature Synthetic Aperture Radar (Mini SAR)
  • j) Moon Mineralogy Mapper (M3)
  • k) Radiation Dose Monitor (RADOM)

Launch Site SDSC, SHAR, Sriharikota
